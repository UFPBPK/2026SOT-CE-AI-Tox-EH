# 2026SOT-CE-AI-Tox-EH
This repository contains the raw data and code files for relevant presentations in the 2026 Society of Toxicology (SOT) Continuing Education (CE) course AM03: Applications of machine learning and artificial intelligence approaches to advance toxicology and environmental health sciences.

Course Chair: Zhoumeng Lin, University of Florida, Gainesville, FL

Course Co-Chair: Nicole C. Kleinstreuer, National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences (NIEHS), NTP Interagency Center for the Evaluation of Alternative Toxicological Methods (NICEATM), National Institutes of Health, Research Triangle Park, NC

Primary endorser: Computational Toxicology Specialty Section

Other Endorsers: Biological Modeling Specialty Section and Risk Assessment Specialty Section

Presentation #1: Machine Learning Approaches for ADME Prediction to Support Rational Drug Design

Presenter #1: Wei-Chun Chou, University of California, Riverside, CA

Presentation #2: Development of machine learning-powered PBPK models for chemicals and nanoparticles

Presenter #2: Zhoumeng Lin, University of Florida, Gainesville, FL

Presentation #3: Leveraging cell painting morphological profiles for machine learning–driven bioactivity prediction

Presenter #3: Srijit Seal, Human Chemical Company, San Francisco, CA

Presentation #4: Machine Learning models to predict developmental toxicity

Presenter #4: Qunshun Jia, Procter & Gamble, Cincinnati, OH

Presentation #5: Leveraging machine learning for human health risk assessment of environmental pollutants: Case examples of carcinogenicity prediction

Presenter #5: Chi-Yun Chen, University of Florida, Gainesville, FL

Presentation #6: Generative artificial intelligence for accessible research translation in toxicology and environmental health and the ethical considerations

Presenter #6: Ted Smith, University of Louisville, Louisville, KY

Note that only the first 5 presentations contain data and code files deposited in this repository. The 6th presentation does not have any code files.
